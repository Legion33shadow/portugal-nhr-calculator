Frequently Asked Questions

Is NHR still available in 2026?

The original NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) regime closed to new applicants on December 31, 2023. It was replaced by IFICI (Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação) in 2024. IFICI maintains the core 20% flat tax for qualifying categories: high value-added professions (IT, engineering, medicine, finance), registered researchers, remote workers on D8 visa, and qualifying retirees. People who registered under original NHR before 2024 keep their status for the full 10-year period.

Who qualifies for IFICI in 2026?

Qualifying categories: (1) High value-added professionals in approved sectors — tech, engineering, R&D, finance, architecture, medicine; (2) Researchers and academics registered with approved Portuguese institutions; (3) Remote workers holding a valid D8 visa (minimum income $3,280/month in 2026); (4) Qualifying retirees with foreign pensions taxed at 10%. You must establish Portuguese tax residency (183+ days OR habitual residence).

What is the D8 digital nomad visa?

The Portugal D8 visa (launched 2022) allows remote workers and freelancers to live legally in Portugal while working for foreign clients/employers. Requirements: proof of stable income ≥ $3,280/month (4× Portuguese minimum wage), health insurance, clean criminal record, accommodation proof. Processing time 2026: 2–4 months. Once in Portugal 183+ days, you qualify for IFICI tax residency.

Are foreign pensions taxed under IFICI?

Foreign pension income is taxed at 10% under IFICI (changed from 0% under the original NHR after 2020 reforms). However, some bilateral tax treaties may allocate taxation rights to the source country (e.g., France, Italy have specific clauses). For UK pensions: HMRC typically retains some taxation rights. Always verify with a Portuguese Contabilista Certificado and check your country's double taxation treaty with Portugal.

What does the transition from NHR to IFICI change?

Key changes: (1) Qualifying professions are now explicitly listed vs the broad original NHR; (2) Pension income: 0% → 10%; (3) Foreign passive income (dividends, capital gains): no longer automatically exempt — depends on origin country and treaty; (4) Application process now requires pre-approval from the relevant Portuguese ministry before tax registration. The 20% flat rate on Portuguese-sourced qualifying income is unchanged.

How accurate is this comparison?

Home country effective rates use widely published estimates for standard income levels in each country. They are accurate as reference points but individual situations vary (married, children, deductions, specific employment contracts). IFICI rates (20% flat or 10% pension) are published by AT (Autoridade Tributária). For complex situations or high net worth, always consult a bilingual tax advisor specializing in international mobility.

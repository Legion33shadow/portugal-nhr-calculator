Frequently Asked Questions

Is NHR still available in 2026?

The original NHR closed to new applicants on December 31, 2023. It was replaced by IFICI (Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação). IFICI maintains the 20% flat tax for qualifying professions and remote workers on the D8 visa, and 10% for qualifying retirees. People registered under original NHR before 2024 keep their full 10-year status.

Who qualifies for IFICI in 2026?

Qualifying categories: (1) High value-added professionals in approved sectors — IT, engineering, R&D, finance, architecture, medicine; (2) Researchers registered with approved Portuguese institutions; (3) Remote workers on a valid D8 visa (income ≥ €3,280/month); (4) Qualifying retirees with foreign pensions (taxed at 10%). You must establish Portuguese tax residency (183+ days or habitual residence).

What is the D8 digital nomad visa?

Launched in 2022, the D8 allows remote workers and freelancers to live legally in Portugal while working for foreign clients or employers. Requirements: documented stable income ≥ €3,280/month, health insurance, clean criminal record, accommodation proof. Processing 2026: 2–4 months. After 183+ days in Portugal, you qualify for IFICI tax residency.

Are foreign pensions taxed under IFICI?

Foreign pension income is taxed at 10% under IFICI (changed from 0% under original NHR after 2020). Some bilateral tax treaties may allocate taxation rights to the source country — French, Italian and UK government pensions have specific treaty clauses. Always verify with a Portuguese Contabilista Certificado before committing.

How accurate is this comparison?

Home country effective rates use widely published estimates for standard income levels. IFICI rates (20% flat or 10% pension) are published by AT (Autoridade Tributária). Individual situations vary by marital status, children, deductions and employment structure. For complex situations or high net worth, consult a bilingual tax advisor specializing in international mobility.

What does Portugal cost of living look like in 2026?

Lisbon: €2,000–2,800/month. Porto: €1,500–2,200. Algarve: €1,600–2,500. Interior cities (Braga, Coimbra): €1,100–1,700. For most Northern European or US earners above €60,000/year, the combination of lower taxes and lower cost of living creates significant net financial gain within 12–18 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NHR still available in 2026?

The original NHR regime closed to new applicants in 2024. It was replaced by IFICI (Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação). IFICI targets qualifying professionals (tech, R&D, finance, startups), remote workers on the D8 visa, and EU retirees with foreign pensions. The core benefit — 20% flat tax — remains for eligible categories.

Who qualifies for IFICI in 2026?

Qualifying categories include: high value-added professionals (IT, engineering, medicine, finance, architecture), registered researchers and academics, investors under specific programs, remote workers holding a valid Portuguese D8 visa, and certain EU/EEA pension recipients. You must become a Portuguese tax resident (183+ days or habitual residence).

Are foreign pensions taxed in Portugal under IFICI?

Foreign pensions are generally taxed at 10% under the IFICI retirement category (changed from 0% under old NHR in 2020). However, double taxation treaties with your origin country may affect this. Some countries (France, Italy) have specific pension clauses. Always verify with a Portuguese tax lawyer.

How long does the IFICI benefit last?

Like the original NHR, IFICI grants preferential tax status for 10 consecutive years. After this period, you are taxed at standard Portuguese progressive rates (up to 48% for high incomes).

What is the cost of living in Portugal vs savings?

Lisbon: €1,800–2,800/month (rent + expenses). Porto: €1,400–2,200/month. Algarve: €1,600–2,600/month. For most Northern European or US earners above €60,000/yr, the combination of lower taxes + lower cost of living creates significant net financial gain within 12–18 months.

Do I still pay social security in Portugal?

Yes. Portuguese social security applies if you are working locally (11% employee contribution). If you remain employed by a foreign company and work remotely, you may contribute to your home country's social security under EU regulations (A1 certificate). This is separate from income tax and is not affected by IFICI status.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NHR still available in 2026?

The original NHR closed to new applicants December 31, 2023. It was replaced by IFICI (Incentivo Fiscal à Investigação Científica e Inovação). IFICI maintains the 20% flat tax for qualifying professions (tech, engineering, R&D, finance, medicine), remote workers on D8 visa, and retirees at 10%. People registered under original NHR before 2024 keep their full 10-year status.

Who qualifies for IFICI?

Qualifying categories: (1) High value-added professionals in approved sectors — IT, engineering, R&D, finance, architecture, medicine; (2) Researchers registered with Portuguese institutions; (3) Remote workers on D8 visa (income ≥ €3,280/month); (4) Retirees with qualifying foreign pensions (taxed 10%). Requirement: Portuguese tax residency (183+ days or habitual residence).

What is the D8 digital nomad visa?

Launched 2022, the D8 visa allows remote workers and freelancers to live legally in Portugal while working for foreign clients or employers. Requirements: stable income ≥ €3,280/month (4× minimum wage), health insurance, clean criminal record, accommodation proof. Processing 2026: 2–4 months. After 183+ days, qualifies for IFICI tax residency.

Are foreign pensions taxed under IFICI?

Foreign pension income is taxed at 10% under IFICI (changed from 0% under original NHR after 2020 reforms). Some bilateral tax treaties may allocate taxation rights to the source country (France, Italy, UK government/civil service pensions have specific clauses). Always verify with a Portuguese Contabilista Certificado and check your country’s double taxation treaty with Portugal before committing.

How accurate is this comparison?

Home country effective rates use widely published estimates for standard income levels. IFICI rates (20% flat or 10% pension) are published by AT. Individual situations vary: married status, children, specific deductions, and employment contract structure all affect your effective rate. For complex situations or high net worth, consult a bilingual tax advisor specializing in international tax mobility.

What does Portugal cost of living look like?

Lisbon (2026): €2,000–2,800/month including rent. Porto: €1,500–2,200/month. Algarve coast: €1,600–2,500/month. Interior cities (Braga, Coimbra, Aveiro): €1,100–1,700/month. For most Northern European or US earners above €60,000/year, the combination of lower taxes plus lower cost of living creates significant net financial gain within 12–18 months of relocation.
